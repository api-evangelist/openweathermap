Steel rails are the backbone of public transport infrastructure, moving millions of passengers daily across vast networks, but that strength carries a specific vulnerability: steel is highly reactive to temperature. In extreme heat, rails expand, and when that expansion exceeds the space available, the track can buckle. OpenWeather examines how weather data and heat resilience science help railway operators proactively manage this risk.
